\question: 16 : Events $E$ and $F$ are such that \pr{E^\prime +F^\prime} = 0.25, State whether $E$ and $F$ are mutually exclusive.\\
\solution :\\
As given in the question ;
 \begin{align} 
	\pr{E^\prime+ F^\prime}&=0.25 \\
	\pr{E^\prime+ F^\prime}&= \pr{EF}^\prime \\
 	\implies 1-\pr{EF}&=0.25 \\
	\implies \pr{EF}&=0.75 
 \end{align}
 \begin{align}
 	\because \pr{EF}\ne 0 
 \end{align}	
		As, the condition of mutually exclusive is 
  \begin{align}
  	\pr{EF}=\emptyset
  \end{align}	 
\begin{center}$\therefore$ $E$ and $F$ are not mutually exclusive events.\end{center}
